## 1. TESTOSTÉRONE TOTALE vs LIBRE - LA DIFFÉRENCE CRUCIALE

### Définitions (Source: Derek/MPMD, Chris Masterjohn PhD)

**Testostérone TOTALE:**
- La quantité TOTALE de testostérone dans le sang
- Inclut TOUTE la testostérone, même celle qui est INUTILISABLE
- Comprend:
  - Testostérone liée à SHBG (60-70%) → **INACTIVE**
  - Testostérone liée à l'albumine (30-38%) → **faiblement active**
  - Testostérone LIBRE (1-3%) → **SEULE FORME ACTIVE**

**Testostérone LIBRE:**
- La testostérone NON liée à une protéine
- **LA SEULE forme biologiquement ACTIVE**
- C'est CETTE forme qui:
  - Active les récepteurs androgéniques dans les muscles
  - Stimule la croissance musculaire
  - Augmente la force
  - Améliore la libido
  - Optimise la performance

### Citation Clé - Derek (More Plates More Dates)

> "You could have a 900 ng/dL total testosterone level and still experience low testosterone symptoms if you don't have an optimal SHBG and free testosterone level. **At the end of the day, free testosterone levels will show you exactly how much testosterone is actually available to be used in tissues.**"

> "If you have high testosterone production, but more of it is being bound up by SHBG and albumin than should be, **it won't matter what your total testosterone level is on paper**."

### Citation Clé - Chris Masterjohn PhD

> "Testosterone is bound in the serum by sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG), and it is thought that **the fraction that is not bound, that is, the free testosterone, is the true measure of what is bioavailable**."

> "You should always use factors such as general well being, motivation, focus, calm confidence under pressure, night-time erections, and libido to track your progress, **not just testosterone levels**."

## 2. SHBG - LA PROTÉINE QUI VOLE VOTRE TESTOSTÉRONE

### Qu'est-ce que SHBG? (Source: Dr. Kyle Gillett/Huberman Lab)

**SHBG = Sex Hormone Binding Globulin**

> "It is the protein that binds up all androgens and estrogen in the body. So the stronger the androgen, the stronger it binds." - Dr. Kyle Gillett

**Le problème:**
- SHBG "séquestre" la testostérone
- La testostérone liée à SHBG = **INACTIVE**
- Plus SHBG est élevé = Moins de testostérone LIBRE
- **SHBG augmente avec l'âge**

### Le Vieillissement et SHBG - Données MPMD/Derek

**Fait alarmant sur le vieillissement:**

Selon Derek (MPMD), voici ce qui arrive avec l'âge:

- Testostérone TOTALE ↓ de **0.8-1.6% par an** après 40 ans
- **MAIS** SHBG ↑ de **1.6% par an**
- **RÉSULTAT:** Testostérone LIBRE (bioavailable) ↓ de **2-3% par an**

> "Not only do natural testosterone levels decrease, but we also have a concurrent **increase in the main protein in the body responsible for binding up usable testosterone and preventing it from transcribing its effects in tissues**."

> "With total testosterone levels decreasing, there is less testosterone in the body, and with an increase in SHBG, **even more of that further declining testosterone gets rendered unusable**."

**C'est DOUBLE PEINE:**
1. Moins de production de testostérone
2. Plus de SHBG qui lie ce qui reste

### Exemple Clinique - Derek/MPMD (Finasteride Gynecomastia Article)

> "Free Testosterone is the only biologically active Testosterone in the body we can actually make use of. **Even if Total Testosterone goes up, if Free Testosterone doesn't go up in parallel to it, you just have a higher level of present Testosterone in the body, but none of that is actually biologically active.**"

Cas du Finasteride:
- Total Testosterone ↑ de 15%
- Mais dans certains cas: Free Testosterone ↓
- Résultat: Symptômes de LOW testosterone MALGRÉ total "normal"

> "**A drop in biologically active Free Testosterone** with a concurrent 15% increase in Estrogen levels in the body can lead to a hormonal imbalance."

## 3. FACTEURS QUI INFLUENCENT SHBG

### Ce qui AUGMENTE SHBG (MAUVAIS = moins de testo libre)

**Sources: Chris Masterjohn PhD, Huberman Lab/Dr. Kyle Gillett**

1. **Vieillissement** - +1.6% par an après 40 ans (MPMD/Derek)
2. **Déficit calorique excessif** (Masterjohn)
3. **Diète low-carb + high-protein** (Masterjohn)
4. **Body fat trop bas** dans certains cas (Masterjohn)
5. **Carence en micronutriments** (Masterjohn)

#### Diète Low-Carb/High-Protein - Citation Masterjohn

> "Studies show that low-carb, high-protein diets cut testosterone by an average of 33%."

Mécanisme: ↑ SHBG → ↓ testostérone libre

### Ce qui DIMINUE SHBG (BON = plus de testo libre)

**Sources: Derek/MPMD, Chris Masterjohn PhD**

1. **Optimiser body fat** (12-17% pour la plupart des hommes)
2. **DHT** (Derek/MPMD - Finasteride article)
3. **Aromatase Inhibitors** dans certains cas (Derek/MPMD)
4. **Nutrition adéquate** - éviter déficit calorique chronique
5. **Certains suppléments** (voir section suivante)

#### Citation Clé - Derek/MPMD (Finasteride Article)

> "**DHT antagonizes SHBG and lowers it**, which is partially why men will use DHT derivatives like Proviron to improve their Free Testosterone levels alongside their Testosterone replacement therapy (TRT)."

> "When SHBG increases, that **increases the amount of Total Testosterone that becomes bound and is no longer bioactive or usable, consequently lowering Free Testosterone**."

5. **Vitamine B2 (Riboflavine)**
6. **Vitamine B3 (Niacine)**

##### Citations Clés - Masterjohn

> "Vitamin D is required to incorporate iron into steroid-producing enzymes correctly."

> "The enzymes that synthesize testosterone also depend on thiamin (B1), riboflavin (B2), niacin (B3), glucose, magnesium and zinc, and are **increased by sodium and chloride (salt)**."

**Protocole Sel - Masterjohn:**

Boron se trouve principalement dans les plantes → **Carnivore diet = potentiellement déficient**

## 6. TESTS SANGUINS - LESQUELS COMMANDER

### Tests OBLIGATOIRES selon Derek/MPMD

**Au MINIMUM, commander:**

1. **Total Testosterone** déterminé par **LC/MS-MS**
   - Pas ECLIA (outdated, imprécis)
   - Liquid Chromatography/Tandem Mass Spectrometry

2. **Free Testosterone** déterminé par **Equilibrium Dialysis OU Equilibrium Ultrafiltration**
   - Pas Direct Analog EIA (garbage)

3. **Estradiol** déterminé par **LC/MS-MS**
   - Pas ECLIA

4. **SHBG** (Sex Hormone Binding Globulin, Serum)

**Bonus utiles:**
- DHT
- DHEA
- Pregnenolone

### Pourquoi Ces Tests Spécifiques?

##### Citation Derek/MPMD - "The Most Accurate Testosterone Blood Test"

> "Recent studies have shown that the current assays used in total testosterone testing **lack the required sensitivity to produce accurate results** when working with very low concentrations."

> "Direct analog enzyme immunoassay (EIA) is inaccurate and **has shown in my own personal tests to detect synthetic anabolic androgenic steroids as testosterone**."

**Exemple personnel de Derek:**
- Nandrolone only (ZÉRO testostérone exogène)
- Test LC/MS-MS: Testostérone CRASHED (correct)
- Test ECLIA/EIA: Testostérone "NORMALE" (FAUX)
- **Le test standard ne pouvait même pas différencier testostérone de 19-nortestosterone**

##### Citation Derek

> "My Testosterone levels were actually in the gutter, but **ECLIA and EIA couldn't even tell the difference between Testosterone and 19-nortestosterone** in my blood."

### Test Exact Recommandé par Derek

**Commander spécifiquement:**

> "Testosterone, Free, Equilibrium Ultrafiltration With Total Testosterone, LC/MS-MS"

### Fréquence des Tests

**Source: Dr. Kyle Gillett (Huberman Lab)**

- **Baseline:** Dès que possible (idéalement à 18 ans)
- **Follow-up:** Tous les **6 mois** si optimisation en cours
- **Maintenance:** Minimum annuel

##### Citation Dr. Gillett

> "Usually, a good follow-up is about six months."

## 7. COMPREHENSION PHYSIOLOGIQUE - Le "Pourquoi" Derrière Tout

### Le Cerveau Calcule Votre Production de Testostérone

**Source: Chris Masterjohn PhD**

##### Citation Fondamentale

> "**Your brain decides how much testosterone you should make** based on the calculation of nutritional abundance, minus energy needed to defend against external and internal emergencies, with a multiplier that adjusts the level up or down according to the life stage you are in."

**Les signaux:**

**Abondance nutritionnelle:**
1. Signal primaire: **Leptine**
2. Signal secondaire: **Insuline**

**Urgences externes:**
- Signal primaire: **Cortisol**

**Urgences internes:**
- Signal primaire: **Inflammation**

### Body Fat = Balancing Act Délicat

##### Citation Masterjohn

> "Body fat and food intake are the main influences on leptin and insulin signaling. We need to have enough body fat and eat enough food. However, **after a certain point, adipose tissue needs to be restructured to accommodate additional fat, and this is considered an internal emergency that elicits inflammation**."

> "Further, **body fat produces the enzyme aromatase, which converts testosterone to estrogen**. Therefore, managing body fat and food intake is a crucial balancing act."

**Le problème du trop de gras:**
- Adipose tissue produit aromatase
- Aromatase convertit testostérone → œstrogène
- Résultat: ↓ testostérone, ↑ œstrogène
